The story so far...

Drawing has always been a passion of mine. For as long as I can remember I've loved to draw Looney Tunes characters. Their cynicism and sharp wit appealed to me from a very young age and I still enjoy watching the originals.

Being an actor never came easy to me, and it certainly wasn't something I was groomed to do growing up. Performing arts classes weren't in our vocabulary, and college wasn't an option. My origin story is deeply rooted in 'stability'. 'Get good grades so you can get a job, and get a check twice a month.' I don't come from a family of business owners, entrepreneurs or entertainers. Starting businesses and taking risks? My upbringing taught me that people like us don't do things like that.

Performing found me - as an escape from a very difficult and abusive childhood. Staying up all hours of the night washing walls, standing at attention or looking for orhpan socks meant that I had plenty of time to use my imagination to keep me awake, and this proved to be fertile ground for a six year old kid growing up in Japan in the 80s.

Making my mom and little brother laugh through the pain of our situation at home led me to share these talents with my classmates, and I never looked back. Eddie Murphy and Richard Pryor kept me company during the long punishing evenings and Yosemite Sam, Foghorn Leghorn and Pepe LePew kept me sane at school. Before I knew it, I was performing at the Wood Junior High School talent show in Davenport Iowa. I did almost 30 minutes off the top of my head and the school jumped to their feet when I was finished. Maybe I wasn't the 'stupid useless nigger' my dad told me I was. 'Still ain't no real job'.

Afraid to step out on my own with no money for college, I joined the family business. The military. On June 6 1992 I graduated from North High School in Davenport Iowa. On June 16 1992 I arrived at Lackland AFB Texas for US Air Force bootcamp. Leaving the hell I knew as 'home' was the happiest day of my life. And I still love to draw.
